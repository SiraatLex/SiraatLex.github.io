What We Do

Our Main Drive

Our main goal is to get the Muslims of Lexington to love Islam. Often times, our relationship with Islam is restricted by a lack of knowledge or a disconnect between those who study the deen for a living and those who are still learning.

Since its inception, Siraat Lexington has hosted over 10 distinct lecturers and speakers, many of whom have spent most of their lives studying Islam in a formal institution. Our members have the opportunity to learn from them and pick their brains on topics that they have never gotten the chance to ask before.

Uniting the Community Under One Umbrella

Siraat Lexington is intentionally designed as a moving organization. Its purpose is to unite people that may be separated by the mosque of choice, age, or occupation.

Our Vision

Background

The Muslim community of Lexington is rapidly growing. For the last ten years, we have seen increasing number of congregants for daily prayers, jummah, and taraweeh prayer. As we grow, the need for interconnectivity amongst Muslims in an Islamic setting is more needed than ever. How can a child, college, or working individual cultivate a relationship with the Mosque, while learning more about Islam?

Siraat Lexington attempts to answer this question by providing an environment filled with comrodory, food, and vibes. At the same time, providing an opportunity to hear from scholars and build real relationships as a teacher and student.

Sisters Suhbah

About the Initiative

Our newest initiative is Sisters Suhbah, a post graduate group that meets once per month. Often times, Muslims that graduate from university find themselves busy with further education, parenthood, employment, and more. This unfortunately results in isolation from peers and a disruptions in social life.

Sisters Suhbah attempts to subvert this phenomenon by providing a space where post-graduate sisters can convene and grow as Muslims. Examples of previous events hosted under this new initiative are:

  • Ramadan Qiyam
  • Dua reflections and Dhikr
  • Pickleball
  • Group watches of Islamic Lecture series'
